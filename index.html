Maya: Illusions in Daily Life

Breaking through the veils of perception

Maya reflects the subtle yet powerful illusions that shape how we see and understand the world. In our everyday lives, perception often outweighs truth, guiding the way we think, act, and respond to the world around us. The beliefs we hold, the stories we follow, and the impressions we form can create realities that feel certain, yet may only be fragments of a larger picture.

Maya takes many forms. It appears in the way we construct our identities, raising questions about whether the self is fixed or shaped by the expectations around us. It colours our relationship with time, making us feel rushed in an age designed for efficiency. It shapes how we connect, where the promise of being constantly linked may hide a sense of distance. It even touches our search for clarity, as we often turn to simple answers in a world that resists them.

By exploring the various notions presented to us, we start the journey to unearth a reality beyond the clutches of Maya and take control of our paradigm.
